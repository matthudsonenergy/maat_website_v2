Why MAAT

Why low-CI fuel fits landfill gas

Built for the gas that landfill sites already produce. MAAT uses landfill methane together with co-located CO2 to make low-CI fuel, turning a fragmented emissions problem into a liquid product with established downstream demand.
Lower power demand than electrolysis-led routes. The pathway comparison indicates roughly 1,800 to 2,000 kWh per ton MeOH equivalent, well below fully electrolysis-based green fuel pathways.
Stronger carbon efficiency from the same site footprint. CO2 utilization in the pathway ranges from 1.0 to 3.0 mol CO2 per mol product, supporting a more efficient carbon conversion story at landfill sites.
Practical first-wave deployment economics. With 54 sites above 5.0 mmscfd and pathway CapEx intensity in the $900 to $1,300 per ton MeOH per year range, the portfolio supports a focused first buildout.
